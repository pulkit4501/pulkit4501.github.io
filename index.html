I AM
I didn’t start in tech.
I started in numbers —
and that changed everything.

My path began at Singapore Management University studying Accountancy — not the most obvious route into AI. But that’s exactly the point. While most technical consultants think in systems and code, I was trained to think in Risk, ROI, and what a CFO cares about at 3am. The Data Analytics second major came later, but the financial lens never left.

What drives me is the space between worlds. A defect-rate problem nobody could solve — until edge AI. A government tender everyone had written off — until someone could make the technical case in a language procurement committees actually trust.

That gap — technical reality meets business ambition — is where I live.

Outside of work, I try my best to stay sharp and grounded. I’ve captained a basketball team, trained Muay Thai, picked up chess, and volunteered with Rainbow Centre and Project Yokefellow. Showing up for people — on and off the court — is something I take seriously.
